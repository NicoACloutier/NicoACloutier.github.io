Welcome to my website :)

My name is Nicolas Cloutier and I am a computer science major studying at the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, PA.

I am interested in computer science and programming, with particular interests in backend web development, functional programming, and natural language processing.

Some things I enjoy are:

  • Programming. I regularly create and commit to open source software projects. Some of my favorite languages are Julia and Haskell.
  • Natural language processing. I enjoy the process of teaching a computer to analyze and understand something as complex as human language.
  • Uto-Aztecan languages. Grammatically, the Uto-Aztecan language family is the most intriguing to me out of any I've come across.
  • Conlangs. I enjoy creating my own languages when I have the time. I primarily make engineered languages, with most of my languages focusing on messing with common verb universals, changing the way they function semantically or syntactically.
  • Mexican folk music. I enjoy son jarocho, huapango, son jalisciense, pirekua, son istmeño, and polka.
  • Discworld novels. Out of those I've read, my favorite is Small Gods, and I'm making my way through the rest.
  • Language learning. I enjoy learning languages. For some time now my focus has been on French and Huasteca Nahuatl.
