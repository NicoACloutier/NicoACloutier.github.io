Welcome to my website :)

My name is Nicolas Cloutier and I am a student in Washington, DC.

I am interested in computer science and linguistics, and particularly in Natural Language Processing (NLP) and computational linguistics, where they intersect.

Some things I enjoy are:

  • Programming. I most commonly use Python and Julia, and regularly create and commit to open source software projects.
  • Natural language processing. I enjoy the process of teaching a computer to analyze and understand something as complex as human language, and wish to pursue this field as my career.
  • Uto-Aztecan languages. Grammatically, the Uto-Aztecan language family is the most intriguing to me out of any I've come across. My introduction to it was Nahuatl, a language I still enjoy very much and wish to learn more about, but I've lately been looking more into studying Tohono O'odham and Rarámuri as well.
  • Conlangs. I enjoy creating my own languages when I have the time. I primarily make engineered languages, with most of my languages focusing on messing with common verb universals, changing the way they function semantically or syntactically.
  • Mexican folk music. I enjoy son jarocho, huapango, son jalisciense, pirekua, son istmeño, and polka.
  • Language learning. I cycle in and out of languages, but for some time now my focus has been on French and Huasteca Nahuatl.
